core_claim: >
  "Network capital" is an existing academic term, distinct from and narrower than social capital,
  coined within the sociology of mobilities. Urry (2007) defines it as the capacity to sustain
  social relationships with people who are not necessarily geographically proximate, and to
  generate emotional, financial, or practical benefit from those relationships - the defining
  feature is the capacity for connection *at a distance*, not physical or organizational closeness.
summary: >
  Developed by John Urry as part of his broader "new mobilities paradigm" in sociology, and
  elaborated with Jonas Larsen and Kay Axhausen in their 2006 collaborative work. The concept
  treats the ability to travel, communicate, and coordinate across distance (enabled by transport
  and communication technology) as itself a form of capital - the capacity-generating infrastructure
  underneath a person's or organization's ability to build and sustain a geographically dispersed
  relationship network. It is explicitly positioned in the literature as an evolution or extension
  of social capital for an era of high mobility and mediated communication, not a fully independent
  tradition.
