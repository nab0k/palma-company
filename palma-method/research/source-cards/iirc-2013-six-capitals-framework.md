summary: >
  The IIRC (established 2010, merged into the Value Reporting Foundation in 2021 and then into
  the IFRS Foundation in 2022) developed the Six Capitals model - financial, manufactured,
  intellectual, human, social and relationship, and natural capital - as "stores of value" that
  serve as inputs to a business model and are transformed through business activities into
  outputs and outcomes. The Six Capitals model was first articulated around 2011 and formalized
  in the International <IR> Framework released in December 2013 (with a revised edition in 2021).
  Its purpose is explicitly to broaden corporate reporting beyond financial capital alone, giving
  companies a standardized vocabulary to disclose non-financial value creation to investors and
  stakeholders.
