core_claim: >
  A generalized moral norm of reciprocity - "people should help those who have helped them" and

summary: >
  Gouldner distinguishes three related but different ideas: (1) reciprocity as a factual pattern of
  mutually contingent exchange (what people actually do); (2) folk beliefs about reciprocity
  ("you scratch my back, I'll scratch yours" as a cultural saying); and (3) the generalized moral
  norm of reciprocity - an internalized obligation to repay benefits received and to avoid harming
  benefactors, backed by social sanction for violation. He argues this moral norm functions as a
  stabilizing mechanism in any ongoing social system because it lowers the transaction cost of
  cooperation: people can extend a benefit without a contract because the norm itself creates an
  expectation of return. Gouldner also introduces the idea that reciprocity obligations are
  calibrated - the value, timing, and form of the "debt" affects how and when repayment is
  expected, and status differences can shift who is expected to initiate.

  original formulation does not fully capture.
