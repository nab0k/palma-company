core_claim: >
  Not all socially meaningful gatherings produce durable community. A **wispy community** is a
  transient, often anonymous or semi-anonymous gathering (a queue, a temporary online thread, a
  one-time crowd) that participants experience as momentarily meaningful and even "communal," but
  that lacks the recurring interaction, shared history, and persistent structure required to sustain
  itself past the gathering itself. Wispy communities are real and can be affectively significant to
  participants without being durable, institutionalizable, or a stepping-stone to a "fuller"
  community.
summary: >
  Fine and van den Scott argue against treating stronger, longer-lasting community forms as the
  natural or superior endpoint of all social gathering. Some social forms are legitimately transient
  by nature - they occur, generate a genuine (if fleeting) sense of shared identity or purpose, and
  then dissolve, without failure and without needing to be "fixed" into something more permanent.
  This directly challenges any organizational framework that treats every gathering, channel, or
  community-like structure as an early-stage version of a durable, maturing community that should be
  cultivated toward permanence.
