## 4. Duplication risks identified

1. **Chapter 3 and the Introduction** both discuss the reputation-vs-access distinction. Chapter 3 is the fuller, five/six-way treatment; the Introduction should eventually be checked to ensure it previews rather than duplicates Chapter 3's full argument.
2. **Chapter 3 and Chapter 6** both touch "distinction from reputation" as part of defining Relationship Capital. Chapter 6 v0.3's technical definition contains a compact version of part of what Chapter 3 now develops in full - a future integration pass should decide which chapter is the primary home for this material.
3. **Chapter 4 and Chapter 9** both discuss "dependence on a few connections" / concentration risk - Chapter 4 introduces it as a cost; Chapter 9 develops the underlying structural mechanism in full. This is intentional layering (cost first, mechanism later) rather than accidental duplication, but should be explicitly cross-referenced in both directions during integration.
4. **Chapter 5 and Chapter 2** both draw on Wenger's communities-of-practice model. Chapter 2 introduces it; Chapter 5 extends it into the event/ritual context. Should remain complementary if cross-referenced rather than re-explained.
5. **Chapter 9 and Chapter 7** both discuss Burt's brokerage/structural-holes research. Chapter 9's draft explicitly defers the strong-tie/weak-tie material to Chapter 7 and does not re-derive Granovetter's finding - confirmed during drafting, should be verified again during a full manuscript read-through.

## 5. Terminology requiring a single consistency pass

- "Access" is used in three related but distinct senses across the manuscript: the original Introduction/Chapter 3 sense (a relationship-level behavioral fact - will this specific party respond), and the Chapter 9 sense (a portfolio-level structural fact - does a bridge exist into a given part of the network at all). Both are legitimate and complementary, but a reader moving from Chapter 3 to Chapter 9 should be told explicitly that the word is being used at a different level of analysis, not just left to infer it.
- "Institutionalization" (Chapter 6) and "institutional memory" (Chapter 5) are related but not identical - the former is the process, DECISION_0003's canonical term; the latter is a specific mechanism/outcome of sustained institutionalization within a recurring relationship system. Chapter 5's draft treats institutional memory as downstream of institutionalization; this relationship should be confirmed rather than assumed once Chapter 6's final wording is locked.
- "Group" and "cluster" (Chapter 9) are used with a specific, stated distinction (formally recognized boundary vs. structurally identifiable density) - this distinction does not yet appear anywhere else in the manuscript and should be checked for consistency if either term recurs in later, not-yet-drafted chapters.
