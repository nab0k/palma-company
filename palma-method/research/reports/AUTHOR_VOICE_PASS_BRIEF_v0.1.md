# Author voice pass brief v0.1

## Trigger

Run this pass only after Serhii supplies the approved author-voice document and confirms it is final enough to govern the book. Draft voice notes are not authorization.

## Scope

The pass is a line edit across the assembled manuscript. It may change cadence, syntax, paragraph order within a local section, transitions, examples already supported by evidence, and the amount of explanation a reader receives.

It may not change:

- the canonical six-phase Method;
- the five Relationship Capital dimensions;
- evidence qualifications or verification limits;
- factual claims, numbers, names, or source scope;
- the distinction between Palma synthesis and academic findings;
- privacy or consent boundaries;
- the prospective status of Collective;
- chapter lineage or approved decisions.

## Priority corrections

1. Remove repeated AI-shaped antithesis, especially mechanical “not X but Y” constructions.
2. Remove throat-clearing such as “it is worth stating,” “the distinction that matters,” and repeated declarations that a claim is precise.
3. Break overlong explanatory sentences where the caveat overwhelms the argument.
4. Keep caveats close to the claim, but move process language out of reader-facing prose.
5. Vary paragraph openings and rhythm.
6. Replace generic executive language with concrete verbs and observable actions.
7. Preserve useful restraint: the voice should sound certain about the Method's decisions and appropriately uncertain about evidence that remains bounded.
8. Make research introductions answer three questions quickly: why this source, what it uniquely shows, and what it cannot establish.
9. Remove any remaining repetition created by the packet-to-manuscript assembly.
10. Protect concise passages; do not lengthen the book merely to make the prose sound literary.

## Required inputs from the Founder

- the approved voice document;
- two or three passages confirmed as positive voice references;
- any prohibited phrases or rhetorical habits not already covered;
- preferred English convention if the publication needs a single house style;
- approval for any personal scene before it enters the manuscript.

## Pass sequence

1. Calibrate on the introduction and one conceptual chapter.
2. Compare the result against the approved samples.
3. Lock a small voice checklist.
4. Edit Parts I–IV and the conclusion in reading order.
5. Run a fact-preservation diff against the pre-voice build.
6. Run a repetition and terminology check.
7. Produce a new versioned build; do not overwrite v0.1.
8. Proof the final build after Collective is inserted or removed.

## Acceptance criteria

- The book reads as one author rather than a sequence of research packets.
- No service labels, version notes, raw placeholders, or task language appear.
- No repeated rhetorical template dominates several chapters.
- Research is introduced with editorial purpose.
- Canonical terms are stable.
- Every qualification survives with the same or stronger meaning.
- Collective remains prospective unless real results have been observed and separately approved.
- The pre-voice and post-voice builds remain traceable.
