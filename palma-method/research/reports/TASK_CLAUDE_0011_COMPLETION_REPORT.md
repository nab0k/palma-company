## 3. Central claim of each chapter

- **Chapter 11 (Listen):** Organizational listening is a distinct capability from speaking, most organizations systematically underinvest in it, and feedback volume must never be read as representativeness.
- **Chapter 12 (Map):** Making relationships, influence, access, and dependency visible requires several distinct, complementary map types (stakeholder, relationship, network, flow), and every map is a provisional representation built on incomplete data, not a finished diagnosis.
- **Chapter 13 (Design):** Designing for relationships requires specifying five distinct system components (channels, roles, rituals, governance, value exchange) together, each resting on an explicit, testable theory-of-change assumption rather than being engineered mechanically.
- **Chapter 14 (Activate):** Activation is coordinated action proceeding through limited, genuinely safe-to-fail interventions with a real sensing loop, succeeding only when ownership transfers from designer/champion to the organization itself.
- **Chapter 15 (Measure):** Measurement exists to support specific decisions, requires distinguishing several routinely-conflated metric pairs, and must never collapse Relationship Capital's five dimensions into one invented universal score.
- **Chapter 16 (Connect):** Communication and community are overlapping but distinct capabilities - communication enables participation, community generates its own knowledge and practice - and Relationship Capital's five dimensions can serve as a shared cross-functional vocabulary without merging the two.
