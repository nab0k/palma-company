# Palma × Collective insertion brief v0.1

## Editorial purpose

Collective should enter the book as a **prospective field note**, not as proof that the Palma Method works. Its distinctive value is temporal: the reader sees a real situation diagnosed before the outcome is known, sees the intervention and evidence criteria stated in advance, and can later follow what happened.

This makes the case fresh and testable without manufacturing certainty. It also creates a legitimate continuation after publication.

## What the case can establish

The book may establish that Palma:

- turns a vague request into a bounded, testable question;
- distinguishes an audience, network, group, and community using observable evidence;
- identifies a specific relationship mechanism and at least one rival explanation;
- helps the client choose an outcome and owner;
- designs a limited intervention with a failure condition;
- states in advance what will be measured and what will not count as proof.

The book may not establish that the Method caused growth, trust, retention, community, or commercial results before those results exist.

## One-week production plan

### 1. Consent and boundaries — 20 minutes

Obtain written agreement from Miguel on:

- use of the Collective name;
- use of his name and role;
- topics that are on and off the record;
- whether direct quotations may be published;
- whether members may be approached;
- the exact Instagram account readers may follow;
- his right to fact-check factual details without rewriting the editorial judgment.

### 2. Founder interview — 60 minutes

Interview Miguel for incidents, not slogans:

1. What is Collective trying to make possible for people?
2. Tell me about the last moment when the group felt genuinely alive without you driving it.
3. Tell me about the last moment when activity stopped after your attention moved elsewhere.
4. Who creates value for whom today?
5. What do members already do for one another?
6. Where does participation depend on you or one other central person?
7. What decision are you avoiding because the evidence is incomplete?
8. What would you be willing to change in the next 30 days?
9. What result would make you stop or redesign the experiment?
10. What would you never permit Palma to turn this group into?

### 3. Member perspective — three interviews of 30 minutes if feasible

Select different participation positions: one core contributor, one occasional participant, and one person who has become less active.

Ask each:

1. What was the last Collective interaction worth returning for?
2. What do you receive here that you cannot get from Collective's public content?
3. When have you contributed value to another member?
4. What makes participation easy, risky, or pointless?
5. What would disappear if Miguel stepped back for a month?
6. What should Collective not formalize or scale?
7. What is one change you would notice within 30 days?

Three interviews do not represent the whole group. They supply contrasting first-hand accounts and expose where Miguel's account converges or diverges.

### 4. Palma working session — 90 minutes

Produce a one-page case record:

| Canonical phase | Book-time output |
|---|---|
| Orient | Situation, mandate, affected people, real question |
| Diagnose | Current social form, mechanism, rival explanation, evidence limits |
| Decide | Required outcome, zone, named owner |
| Design | One bounded intervention, assumptions, participant risk, failure condition |
| Enable | First action and operating owner |
| Evaluate | Baseline, leading signal, lagging signal, continue/change/stop date |

The intervention should be small enough to start within seven days and meaningful enough that failure teaches something. Do not promise a community strategy in full. Choose one live relationship mechanism.

### 5. Fact and consent check — 30 minutes

Miguel confirms names, dates, quotations, confidentiality, and the public follow link. Interviewees approve their quotations individually. Silence is not consent.

## Recommended book form

Length: 1,000–1,500 words.

Placement: after Chapter 21 and before the conclusion.

Structure:

1. **The brief:** what Collective thought it needed.
2. **What the evidence complicated:** the observed social form and the gap.
3. **The decision:** the outcome and owner chosen.
4. **The test:** one intervention, stated assumptions, participant risk, and failure condition.
5. **What is not known yet:** explicit limits at publication.
6. **Follow the result:** approved Instagram link and a note that readers can ask Miguel whether the Method was useful.

Suggested closing logic: the book does not ask the reader to trust a retrospective success story. It publishes the question, the intervention, and the evidence standard before the answer exists.

## Inclusion gate

Include the case only if all are true:

- written consent exists;
- at least one first-hand account beyond Miguel is available, or the absence is stated prominently;
- there is a real tension or decision, not only a flattering profile;
- the intervention and failure condition are specific;
- no result is implied before observation;
- Miguel approves factual accuracy;
- the case can survive removal of promotional language.

If any condition fails, remove the insertion marker. The book remains complete without the case.

## Post-publication continuation

- Publish a short baseline note with the book launch.
- Share one weekly observation on the approved Collective Instagram account without declaring victory.
- At 30 days, publish continue/change/stop.
- At 60–90 days, publish the longer result as an article, video conversation, or second-edition addendum.
- Keep the same predeclared evidence criteria so the continuation cannot quietly redefine success after the fact.
