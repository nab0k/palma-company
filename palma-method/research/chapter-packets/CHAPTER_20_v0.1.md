# Chapter 20 — Advise (packet)

**Maps to core phase:** Evaluate, plus course correction, while ownership stays with the organization (Palma Method Canon v1.0 §3, §6).

## Purpose

Show what it looks like for Palma to keep reading evidence and offering judgment after a design is live, without quietly taking back the decision rights Decide already assigned to the client. Advise is the phase most likely to drift into either extreme: Palma disengaging entirely once a pilot launches, or Palma re-asserting control because the client's own read of the evidence seems slower or less confident than Palma's own.

## Central argument

Evaluate is not a report card; it is a discipline of checking a live design against the layered outcome model, distinguishing what the design contributed from what else might explain a change, and reaching an explicit decision — adapt, continue monitoring, transfer ownership, or stop. What makes this phase "Advise" rather than a repeat of Decide is the ownership boundary: Palma's role is to sharpen the client's own evidence and judgment, not to substitute a fresh Palma judgment for the client's, even when Palma's read of the pattern is more confident. Course correction under this phase means adjusting the design in response to evidence, not Palma quietly resuming the decision authority that belongs, per Decide, to the client.

## Phase mapping

**Evaluate:** evidence, consequences, limits, continue/change/stop/restart (DECISION_0005). Operationally: assess against all five layers of the outcome model (orientation, value clarity, action architecture, collective capacity, adaptive relational capability), not activity or sentiment metrics alone; separate contribution from attribution by naming what else could explain an observed change; check whether the evidence would support the same conclusion for another practitioner reading it cold; make the continue/adapt/handover/stop decision explicit rather than letting the engagement drift forward by default.

## Claims / evidence table

| Claim | Evidentiary basis | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Outcomes should be assessed across multiple distinct layers, not one summary metric | Layered outcome model, built on Pawson & Tilley's realist evaluation logic (context + mechanism → outcome, evaluated per mechanism) | Well-supported methodological principle; the specific five-layer model is Palma's own synthesis |
| Contribution and attribution are separable, and conflating them overstates an intervention's role | Carol Weiss / contribution analysis (theory-of-change discipline in program evaluation) | Well-supported, established evaluation practice |
| Client confidence at the end of an engagement is not itself evidence of a good outcome unless it is grounded in stated evidence and limits | Epistemic vs. performative confidence distinction — Palma's own synthesis, informed by process-consultation literature (Schein) on the risk of consultants producing comfort rather than capability | Reasonable synthesis; not independently tested as a distinguishing measure |
| Fast, visible activity is not the same evidence as durable collective capacity | Tufekci (digital mobilization can be fast and structurally fragile at once) | Well-supported in its own domain (digital/networked mobilization); applied here by extension to organizational evaluation generally |

## Practical tools

The five-layer outcome model (Orientation, Value clarity, Action architecture, Collective capacity, Adaptive relational capability) as the evaluation frame; the explicit continue/adapt/handover/stop decision point, always stated rather than defaulted; Stop Rules re-checked at this phase specifically, since ownership and evidence-access conditions can erode after a design has been live for a while, not only at the point of first commitment.

## Counterarguments

A client under commercial pressure may reasonably read a rigorous, multi-layer evaluation as slower and more expensive than the situation calls for, especially once a pilot already looks like it's working. A second, sharper objection: if Palma is the one applying the layered outcome model and naming rival explanations, how is decision ownership genuinely staying with the client rather than simply being narrated as though it does? The chapter should not resolve this tension rhetorically — it should state plainly that Advise's ownership boundary is a discipline Palma has to actively maintain against its own incentive to look more certain and more useful than the evidence supports, not a boundary that enforces itself.

## Risks of overclaiming

This chapter must not suggest that the layered outcome model, once applied, produces a definitive answer about whether an intervention worked — realist evaluation's own premise is that mechanisms operate differently across contexts, and a finding that held in one moment may not generalize. It must not claim any specific continue/adapt/handover/stop decision was reached in a real engagement, since no citable case is available. It should be explicit that "ownership stays with the organization" is a standard Palma is obligated to hold itself to, not a guaranteed outcome of running the phase.

## Draft structure (for the manuscript prose)

1. Open on the two failure modes: disengaging too early, or re-asserting control under the cover of "just reading the evidence."
2. Evaluate as a discipline across five separate layers, not one verdict.
3. Contribution versus attribution — why a change following an intervention is not the same as a change caused by it.
4. The explicit decision point, and why letting an engagement drift forward by default is itself a decision, just an unexamined one.
5. Naming the ownership tension honestly rather than resolving it rhetorically.

## Sources

Pawson & Tilley (1997); Weiss and contribution-analysis literature; Schein (1999, 2013); Tufekci (2017) — full citations as verified in Palma Canon v0.1 and Core v0.1 §10.

## Verification gaps

The five-layer outcome model has not been validated as a measurement instrument outside this working track's own synthesis. No claim in this chapter about a specific evaluation outcome can be checked against a completed, citable engagement, since the only available case material is restricted.

## Editorial decisions

Written entirely at the level of principle and method; no worked evaluation example is included, since the only real evaluation material available in the working folder sits inside the restricted case file.
