## Chapter 3 — Reputation Is Not Access

| Category | Item |
|---|---|

## Cross-cutting gaps (apply to more than one chapter)

1. **The VI-004 caveat (Barabási-derived hub/resilience findings, established for large networks, not validated at small organizational scale)** applies directly to Chapter 9 and by extension to any future use of the same concept in Chapter 4's "dependence on a few connections" section.
2. **All four chapters contain at least one editorial placeholder** in place of a real, named, verifiable case, consistent with this task's exclusion of invented field observations. None of the four chapters is manuscript-ready until its placeholder is replaced with an approved example.
3. **Three new constructs were grounded for the first time in this wave** (legitimacy - SC-025; retention economics - SC-026; ritual/interaction ritual chains - SC-027) alongside one new network-science card (resilience/attack tolerance - SC-028). None of these four new cards has yet been cross-checked against the existing 24-card base for definitional overlap or contradiction beyond the checks already performed during drafting.
4. **No chapter in this wave required new research to identify a definitional conflict with DECISION_0003's five-dimension model** - all four chapters use trust, reciprocity, access, relationship strength, and network structure consistently with the approved architecture, and none proposes a sixth dimension.

## Summary

Four chapters produced, each with one editorial placeholder pending a real case, one or two claims explicitly labeled as reasoned extensions rather than direct findings, and one unresolved definitional term flagged for a possible future research pass (awareness, repeat participation, institutional memory, and centrality-measure specificity, respectively). No claim in any of the four chapters is presented without a source-card citation or an explicit interpretation label.
