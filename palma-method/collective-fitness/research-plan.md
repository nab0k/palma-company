# Research Plan

Status: draft  
Owner: Serhii Nabok  
Research window: two weeks

## Primary question

What existing relationship structures make The Collective valuable, where do interactions stop, and which small intervention might invite meaningful response?

## Discovery methods

- Deep interview with Miguel.
- Selected member interviews.
- Observation of classes and post-class interaction.
- Website, social channel, and communication audit.
- Member-journey and relationship mapping.

## Discovery outputs

- Existing roles and rituals.
- Informal leaders and visible or invisible subgroups.
- Reasons people join, return, contribute, or withdraw.
- Current value exchanged between participants.
- Interaction gaps and missing bridges.
- Candidate hypotheses for small interventions.

## Intervention selection criteria

- Directly responds to an observed relationship gap.
- Small enough to test within the sprint.
- Requires informed organizational permission.
- Creates no pressure to disclose personal information.
- Produces observable behavior or qualitative feedback.
- Can be stopped or adapted with low participant cost.

## Limits

The study can illustrate diagnosis and early activation. It cannot establish long-term causality, retention impact, financial ROI, or organizational transformation.
