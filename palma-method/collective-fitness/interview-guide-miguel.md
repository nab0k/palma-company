# Interview Guide — Miguel

Duration: approximately 60–75 minutes  
Format: semi-structured  
Status: draft

## Opening

- Confirm purpose, consent, recording preference, confidentiality, and permitted use.
- Ask Miguel to describe The Collective in his own language before introducing Palma terminology.

## Origin and purpose

1. Why was The Collective created?
2. What problem was it meant to solve for members and for the business?
3. What has changed since the beginning?

## Member reality

4. Who joins, who stays, and who leaves?
5. What do members say they value? What does their behavior suggest they value?
6. What happens before, during, and after a class?
7. Where do members interact without staff prompting?

## Relationships and structure

8. Which people naturally welcome, connect, teach, or encourage others?
9. Are there visible subgroups or people who remain at the edge?
10. What rituals already exist?
11. Where does interaction stop even when you wish it continued?

## Communication

12. Which messages receive a response, and which are mostly consumed passively?
13. What happens after an event, announcement, or member milestone?
14. Which communication or community efforts have disappointed you?

## Business and boundaries

15. What business outcomes matter, and over what time horizon?
16. What must this project not disrupt?
17. What information, people, or situations must remain private?

## Experiments

18. If we could test one small change, where would you start?
19. What response would count as meaningful?
20. What result would tell us to stop or change direction?

## Closing

21. What important question have I not asked?
22. Who else sees this system differently and should be interviewed?
23. Confirm next steps and permissions.
