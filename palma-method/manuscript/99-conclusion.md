## Conclusion

Six phases, run in order and then, almost always, run again out of order as evidence demands: orient, diagnose, decide, design, enable, evaluate. That sequence, more than any particular finding it has ever produced, is what this book has actually been arguing for. A discipline that insists on testing a request before acting on it, on naming a rival explanation before trusting a mechanism, on stating in advance what would prove a design wrong, on checking twice whether ownership has genuinely transferred rather than assuming it has — that discipline is the thing worth taking from these pages, more than any specific technique nested inside it.

It is worth being plain, at the end, about what this discipline does not promise, because the temptation to round up is strongest exactly here, in the last few pages a reader is likely to remember. It does not promise that a well-designed condition will produce the trust, belonging, or collective spirit it was hoped to enable — those emerge, if they emerge at all, from what people actually do with the conditions they've been given, and that is not something any design fully controls. It does not promise that a diagnosis, however careful, is the same thing as a correct answer. And it does not promise that community, in the fuller sense this book has used the word, is what any given organization actually needs. Very often it isn't. A method that could only justify its own existence by finding a reason, in every engagement, to recommend the thing it is best known for building would not be a diagnostic method at all — it would be a sales pitch that had learned to speak in diagnostic language. Saying plainly that community is sometimes the wrong answer is not a hedge tucked in at the close. It is part of what makes the rest of this book worth trusting.

A reasonable objection remains, and it deserves a direct answer rather than a dismissal. If the honest core of this method is a willingness to say "it depends" — the social form might be a network rather than a community, the right level of analysis might be one relationship rather than an entire organization, the right decision might be to advise and step back rather than to design and implement — then why does any of this need six named phases, a decision tree, and a shelf of supporting artifacts? Isn't "it depends" already what any careful consultant would say? It is, and that is exactly the problem the structure exists to solve. "It depends" is also where a great deal of communication and relationship work quietly stops being rigorous, because it is a comfortable place to stop asking questions. Naming the phases, the artifacts, and the conditions under which an engagement should pause or refuse is what keeps "it depends" from becoming a polite way of saying "we didn't really check."

One direction this method has not yet been tested in deserves to be named honestly rather than left as a silent gap: its application to genuinely collective, non-commercial settings — communities organized around a shared cause or identity rather than a paying client relationship. Nothing in this book describes an interview conducted, an observation made, or a result reached in such a setting, because none has been undertaken under this method as documented here. That remains a prospective direction, not a completed chapter, and the six-phase architecture this book has described does not depend on that direction ever being taken up. It is named here as an open field, not as a promise.

What this book leaves the reader with, finally, is not a guarantee that any of this works on the first attempt, or the tenth. It is a way of asking, at every stage of a relationship or communication problem, whether the confidence being offered is actually earned — by evidence, by a stated alternative that was seriously considered, by a plan that says in advance what would prove it wrong — or whether it is simply confidence, offered because confidence is persuasive and uncertainty is not. The standard this method should be judged against is not whether it produced a satisfied client at the end of an engagement. It is whether the organization, afterward, is more able to ask these same questions of itself, the next time a request arrives dressed as a solution and asking, politely, not to be questioned.
