# DECISION_0005 — Palma Method core architecture

## Decision

The canonical core logic of Palma Method has six phases:

1. **Orient** — define the situation, boundary, mandate, people affected, and real question.
2. **Diagnose** — establish the relationship reality and the gap between stated and observed conditions.
3. **Decide** — choose the relationship outcome actually required, including when community is not the right goal.
4. **Design** — specify conditions, roles, channels, rituals, governance, value exchange, and testable assumptions without claiming to manufacture social outcomes.
5. **Enable** — launch bounded interventions, support participation, distribute capability, and adapt during use.
6. **Evaluate** — assess evidence, consequences, limits, and whether to continue, change, stop, or restart the cycle.

The phases are an iterative reasoning architecture, not a guaranteed linear transformation sequence.

## Relationship to Part IV

The existing Part IV chapter route remains useful to readers as a client-engagement narrative and does not constitute a competing Method:

- Chapter 17, **Diagnose**, covers Orient and Diagnose.
- Chapter 18, **Design**, covers Decide and Design.
- Chapter 19, **Launch**, covers Enable.
- Chapter 20, **Advise**, covers Evaluate and course correction while ownership remains with the organization.
- Chapter 21, **Handover**, explains the ownership and capability principle that runs through Enable and Evaluate; Handover is not a seventh phase.

## Superseded and preserved language

- The Notion sequence `Diagnose → Audit → Align → Activate → Learn` is a prior working vocabulary and is not canonical.
- The sequence `Diagnose → Design → Launch → Advise → Handover` remains the book's client-engagement route, not the Method core.
- Claude's six-phase full-text draft is the source draft for canonicalization, but no wording that conflicts with approved repository decisions becomes authoritative automatically.

## Relationship Capital constraint

DECISION_0003 remains authoritative. The Method must use the approved Relationship Capital architecture and must not introduce a competing definition, provisional replacement, or sixth dimension.

## Field use and evidence

Collective may be described as the first public field application of the canonical Method. It is not evidence that the Method works until observations exist and are assessed. A pre-publication Collective text may document only baseline, diagnosis, decisions, and prospective hypotheses.

The private Sempre Fixe case test and its underlying meeting material remain restricted and excluded from the book and public Method materials unless separately authorized.
