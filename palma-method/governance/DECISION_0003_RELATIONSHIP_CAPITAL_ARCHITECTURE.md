# DECISION_0003 — Relationship Capital Architecture

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Date:** 2026-07-16  
**Owner:** Serhii  
**Project:** The Palma Method / Palma.company

## Decision

> **Relationship Capital is the value accumulated in an organization’s relationships that enables trust, cooperation, access, support, and coordinated action.**

Russian working translation:

> **Капитал отношений — это накопленная в отношениях организации ценность, которая позволяет получать доверие, сотрудничество, доступ, поддержку и возможность совместно действовать.**

## Canonical architecture

**Relationship-building capabilities and conduct**  
↓  
**Relationships**  
↓  
**Accumulated relational value**  
↓  
**Mobilizability in a specific situation**  
↓  
**Actions and outcomes**

## Distinctions

### Relationship Capital
The accumulated value present in relationships.

It is not identical to personality traits, reputation, audience size, contact databases, communication output, or business outcomes.

### Relationship-building capability
The ability to create, maintain, deepen, and extend valuable relationships.

Relevant inputs may include empathy, honesty, consistency, curiosity, willingness to help, reliability, shared values, and competent relationship stewardship.

### Institutionalization
The transfer or extension of relational value from an individual relationship into an organizational relationship.

Examples:
- trust in a founder extending to the founder’s company;
- trust in a company extending to a new employee;
- an organization retaining relational value after personnel changes.

Institutionalization is a process, not part of the core definition.

### Mobilizability
The degree to which relational value can be converted into coordinated action in a specific situation.

Mobilizability is derived, not a standalone core dimension.

## Core dimensions

1. Trust
2. Reciprocity
3. Access
4. Relationship strength
5. Network structure

## Deferred concepts

Institutional reach and status are not core dimensions at this stage. They may be treated as contextual variables, indicators, resources, modules, or outcomes.

## Unit of analysis

- **Dyadic level:** a relationship between an organization or representative and a specific person or group.
- **Portfolio level:** the organization’s wider system of relationships and the structure formed by them.

## Evidence standard

This definition is a Palma Method synthesis, not a claim of universal academic consensus.
