# Approved Architecture

## Layer 1 — Relationship system

The asset is the set and structure of identifiable relationships in which an organization participates.

The organization does not own the people or the relationships.

## Layer 2 — Dimensions

Approved core dimensions:

1. Trust
2. Reciprocity
3. Access
4. Relationship strength
5. Network structure

## Layer 3 — Mobilizability

Mobilizability is a derived, purpose-specific capacity to activate the relationship system.

It is not a sixth dimension and is not an outcome.

## Layer 4 — Outcomes

Outcomes include:

- information;
- introductions;
- cooperation;
- legitimacy;
- support;
- coordinated action;
- commercial or social results.

Outcomes are not part of Relationship Capital itself.

# Definition Decision

Claude’s proposed short definition:

> Relationship Capital is the system of relationships an organization holds with the people and groups it depends on.

is not approved verbatim.

## Problems

### “holds”

This risks implying ownership or unilateral control.

### “depends on”

This is too narrow. It may exclude:

- weak ties;
- latent ties;
- prospective relationships;
- bridges into new groups;
- relationships whose value is optional rather than necessary.

## Recommended short definition

> **Relationship Capital is the system of identifiable relationships in which an organization participates and may rely on.**

This wording:

- avoids ownership;
- includes active and latent ties;
- allows weak and strong relationships;
- distinguishes relationships from anonymous audiences;
- preserves mobilizability without building outcomes into the definition.

# Dimension Decision

## Approved

- Trust
- Reciprocity
- Access
- Relationship strength
- Network structure

## Deferred as an independent dimension

**Institutional reach**

Current evidence does not yet justify treating institutional reach as a separate universal dimension.

For the current model it should be treated as:

- an indicator of network structure;
- an indicator of access;
- or a domain-specific module for institutional and professional communities.

It may later become a standalone dimension if empirical fieldwork shows that it behaves independently.

# Unit of Analysis

Approved dual-level model:

## Dyadic level

- trust;
- reciprocity;
- access;
- relationship strength.

## Portfolio level

- network structure;
- concentration;
- diversity;
- bridging;
- redundancy.

The organization is the managerial vantage point, but the relationships remain reciprocal and co-produced.

---

# Status Question

Status should not currently become a core dimension.

It may function as:

- a contextual resource that influences access;
- a signal affecting trust and legitimacy;
- an outcome produced by relationship systems;
- a network-position effect.

Chapter 8 must treat status separately rather than forcing it into the core measurement model.
