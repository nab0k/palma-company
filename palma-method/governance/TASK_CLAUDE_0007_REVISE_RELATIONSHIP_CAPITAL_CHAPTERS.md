## Canonical definition

> Relationship Capital is the value accumulated in an organization’s relationships that enables trust, cooperation, access, support, and coordinated action.

Do not silently replace it.

## Required distinctions

Maintain:

1. relationship-building capabilities and conduct;
2. relationships;
3. accumulated relational value;
4. mobilizability;
5. actions and outcomes.

Do not treat empathy, charisma, honesty, or similar traits as Relationship Capital itself.

Treat institutionalization as a process. Treat mobilizability as derived.

Use only five current core dimensions:

- trust;
- reciprocity;
- access;
- relationship strength;
- network structure.

Do not present institutional reach or status as settled core dimensions.
