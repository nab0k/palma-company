Two points in the prior working material were not aligned with canonical governance. Both are corrected here; nothing else moves.

## 1. What Palma is

> Palma makes collective action an object of design.

Palma is a strategic diagnostic and design method for situations where communication, relationships, participation, and coordinated action are materially connected to a strategic problem. It diagnoses the actual social form and mechanism behind a communication, relationship, or participation request, then designs the bounded conditions — access, roles, interaction, recognition, feedback, memory, boundaries, conflict and repair, responsibility transfer — that may enable appropriate collective action. What emerges from those conditions (trust, identity, solidarity, community) is observed, not manufactured. Palma does not design human behavior, trust, or belonging directly, and does not promise them.

Client-facing short version: Palma helps organizations understand what is really blocking communication, participation, and cooperation, then designs the right conditions to address it.

This working definition is unchanged by DECISION_0005; the Decision fixes how the method moves through time (§3 below), not what it is for.

## 2. Relationship Capital (per DECISION_0003 — canonical, not provisional)

**Definition, verbatim from DECISION_0003:** Relationship Capital is the value accumulated in an organization's relationships that enables trust, cooperation, access, support, and coordinated action.

**Architecture:** capabilities and conduct that build relationships → relationships → accumulated relational value → situational mobilizability → actions and outcomes. Mobilizability is a *derived* property of the accumulated value, not a sixth core dimension, and not itself Relationship Capital.

**Five core dimensions, and only these five:** trust, reciprocity, access, relationship strength, network structure. No sixth dimension should be introduced without a Decision that revises DECISION_0003; this document does not attempt to add one.

**What Relationship Capital is not.** Empathy, charisma, and honesty are relationship-building *conduct* — inputs to the first stage of the architecture — not Relationship Capital itself. Collapsing conduct into the capital it produces is the specific error DECISION_0003 was written to close off, and this Canon carries that distinction forward without softening it.

**How this changes the rest of the working material.** Relationship Capability (Core v0.1 §4 — "the organization's repeatable ability to identify, initiate, maintain, repair, and appropriately mobilize relationships") is not redefined by this correction; it sits at the *capabilities and conduct* stage of DECISION_0003's architecture, upstream of Relationship Capital itself, and the two terms should now be read as adjacent stages of one chain rather than loosely related synonyms. Adaptive Relational Capability (Core v0.1 §4, §10 Layer 5) is a further capacity — the ability to reshape the whole chain over time — and is unaffected. Everywhere Core v0.1, the Decision Tree, the Six Artifacts, or a Practice Layer refers to "relationship capital," "trust and reciprocity," or similar language informally, it should now be read against this five-dimension architecture rather than as an open construct. No document in the working folder claimed a sixth dimension or treated empathy/charisma as capital directly, so no artifact requires a line-by-line rewrite — this is a status correction, not a content contradiction.

**Explicit scope caveat, carried from DECISION_0003:** this is a Palma synthesis for operational use, not a claim of universal academic consensus. Practitioners should not present the five-dimension model to a client or reader as the single settled academic account of relational value; it is Palma's working architecture, stated plainly as such.

## 3. The six phases (per DECISION_0005 — canonical)

Palma Method core has six iterative phases. The definitions below are DECISION_0005's; the elaboration under each is this working track's operational material, unchanged in substance from Protocol v0.1 and compatible with the Decision's own language.

**1. Orient** — situation, boundary, mandate, affected people, the real question. Convert the raw request into a testable hypothesis; state, without committing to it, the provisional level of analysis; treat the framing conversation itself as a negotiation over what the engagement is about, before scope hardens.

**2. Diagnose** — relationship reality and the gap between stated and observed conditions. Determine the social form actually present (never accept a "community" label as given), assign the smallest sufficient unit of analysis, build the Relational and Interaction Map with real relationship content, and state at least one rival explanation for the observed problem before accepting a mechanism.

**3. Decide** — choose the relationship outcome actually required, including when community is not the goal. Turn the diagnosis into a bounded decision: which zone (core execution, advisory-only, out of scope), which mechanism, which owner. Decision responsibility belongs to the client, always; Palma proposes.

**4. Design** — conditions, roles, channels, rituals, governance, value exchange, testable assumptions; never claim to manufacture social outcomes. Build the sequenced, owned, testable Action Architecture. State what evidence would show the design is wrong before finalizing it, not after.

**5. Enable** — bounded interventions, participation, distributed capability, adaptation in use. Transfer capability, not just a plan; launch as pilots where feasible; verify both change commitment (shown through action) and change efficacy (the client can explain, unprompted, why a specific action works) before treating this phase as complete.

**6. Evaluate** — evidence, consequences, limits, continue/change/stop/restart. Assess against the layered outcome model, distinguish contribution from attribution, and make an explicit decision rather than a default continuation.

**Iterative, not linear.** DECISION_0005 calls these six phases iterative, consistent with the Decision Tree's own loops (escalate/de-escalate within Diagnose; a Design overreach sending the engagement back to the social-form finding; Evaluate reopening Diagnose when evidence contradicts the working model). A real engagement crossing this cycle more than once is the method working as intended, not a sign of failure.

**Cross-phase governance, unchanged:** Stop Rules and Ethical Limits can trigger at any phase and override progression; de-escalation to a narrower scope is always available and is not a failure of the method.

It does not re-argue Relationship Capital's dimensions, re-derive the six phases, or introduce new theoretical pillars.
