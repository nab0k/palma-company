# Palma prose-quality policy — draft

## Purpose

Protect meaning, evidence, privacy, and a recognizable human voice while
removing formulaic or inflated prose. This draft does not define Serhii Nabok's
personal voice and does not authorize manuscript editing.

## Three layers

### 1. Universal quality

Prose should identify actors, name sources, distinguish evidence from inference,
remove empty repetition, and match the certainty of the underlying material.
Claims, numbers, definitions, citations, and limitations must survive style
editing unchanged unless the editor explicitly authorizes a substantive edit.

### 2. Palma house style

Write for an intelligent business reader without academic fog or promotional
hype. Prefer concrete mechanisms and observed consequences. Use Palma's approved
definitions consistently. Keep technical terms when they carry precise meaning.
Examples must clarify the mechanism and remain traceable or explicitly abstract.

### 3. Author voice

Reserved. It may be documented only from samples the author explicitly approves
for voice calibration. Agents may not infer the author's opinions, humor,
vulnerability, biography, or rhythm from private conversations.

## Privacy rule

Personal stories from conversations are unavailable for manuscript or agent use
unless the author explicitly approves them. Until then, use published evidence,
an abstract non-identifying example, or an editorial placeholder. Approval must
be traceable and specific to the intended use.

## Diagnostic checklist

Before proposing a style edit, ask:

1. Is every factual assertion supported or marked for verification?
2. Did the edit preserve qualifications, causal direction, numbers, and scope?
3. Is the actor clear, or is an abstraction performing a human action?
4. Is any importance claim earned by evidence?
5. Does a paragraph repeat rather than advance the argument?
6. Are sentence and paragraph patterns mechanical enough to distract?
7. Is a flagged feature actually deliberate rhythm, domain language, or useful repetition?
8. Did the edit add opinion, anecdote, informality, or certainty not present in the source?
9. Does any example reveal or derive from an unapproved personal story?
10. Can the editor explain the change without appealing to an “AI score”?

## Editing protocol

Run evidence and privacy checks before style review. Diagnose with rule IDs and
show proposed changes; do not perform an opaque full rewrite. After editing,
compare the original and revision for claims, definitions, citations,
qualifications, and permissions. Escalate any semantic difference to the Chief
Editor.

## Context-sensitive features

Passive voice, fragments, repetition, rhetorical questions, technical terms,
em dashes, and three-part structures are not defects by themselves. They may be
changed when they obscure meaning or become mechanical, not to satisfy a ban or
numeric quota.

## Prohibitions

Agents and tools must not:

- fabricate lived experience, quotations, opinions, or uncertainty;
- inject private names or anecdotes;
- convert inference into fact or weaken verification language;
- rewrite canonical definitions during a style pass;
- optimize prose for an AI detector;
- force generic informality or uniform sentence variation;
- install or apply an external writing skill without approval.

## Acceptance

A chapter passes prose review when the Chief Editor confirms meaning and
evidence preservation, privacy compliance, house-style fit, and deliberate
rather than mechanical rhythm. The Founder retains final authority over voice
and personal material.
