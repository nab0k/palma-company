01 / The real task

The tool is rarely the task.

The brief tells us where to start looking.

The stated request
“We need a video.”

What must this make possible?

A film may be useful. So may PR, a new identity or a LinkedIn strategy. First we need to know which door should open, whose mind should change, or what the audience should actually do.

If nothing changes, attention stays attention. Banks stay cautious. The next market stays closed.

02 / Palma Method

Five questions before anything gets made.

My first conversations with an owner are about the business: what has changed, who matters and what is stuck. The brief usually looks different afterwards. That is when we decide what to make.

01What is really changing?

The owner’s ambition, the business transition and the opportunity that communication must help unlock.

02Who needs to respond?

The audiences, institutions and people whose perception, behaviour or decision matters.

03What stands in the way?

The contradictions, missing relationships, unclear offers and hidden assumptions inside the system.

04What must be decided?

Priorities, positioning, narrative, roles and the choices the organisation has postponed.

05What must be built?

The communication system, team, products and practices that can create a measurable response.

03 / Palma Test

Do you have a community — or only an audience?

Here is what this way of working looks like in practice.

A client says, “We need a community.” That can mean customers, followers, members, partners — or simply people receiving the same newsletter. Before deciding what to build, I ask six questions about the relationship already there.

Those questions became the Palma Test.

Does it clap back?

What do people receive in return for their participation?

Who gets to say “we”?

Is there a real inside — a place where people can belong without becoming identical?

Can people change what happens?

Can members contribute, lead and influence the system?

What happens again and again?

Is there a rhythm, ritual or shared practice people can rely on?

Can disagreement survive?

Can people challenge the organisation and still belong?

Would both sides miss it?

If the relationship disappeared, would both sides lose something that matters?

05 / Palma Assembly

When a decision needs to become something real.

If the answer is a film, I bring in filmmakers. If it is an identity, I bring in designers. Research, writing, digital products, PR or community — the task decides the team.

Most of these people are in Ukraine. All are senior. This gives clients access to a level of thinking and craft that usually requires a much larger Western European agency.

I put the team together and stay with the work from the first conversation to the final delivery.
