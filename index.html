The name
The name is the brief.

In Portuguese, palma means three things at once. The middle one — applause — is the reaction everything else is for.

1
Palm of a hand
na palma da mão — to know it completely
→ Strategy. Your market, your members, your next move — held in the palm of your hand.
2
Applause
palmas — the sound a crowd makes when it means it
→ Community. Customers who clap for you, and a brand smart enough to clap back. This is the reaction.
3
Victor's palm
levar a palma — to take the prize
→ Ideas. The ones worth shipping.

Strategy and ideas are the setup. Applause is the reaction — and the reaction is the strategy.

Who's behind Palma
It's one person. On purpose.

The person you meet is the person who does the work.

Most agencies sell you a pitch team, then hand the actual project to whoever's free. Palma is the opposite — every audit, every interview, every slide is mine. That's the point of hiring one senior strategist instead of an org chart: no telephone game, no juniors learning on your budget.

And "senior" isn't a word on a slide. I've worked in communications since 2005 — from copywriter to, in 2021, strategic-communications adviser to Ukraine's deputy minister of defence, with NATO, the WHO and the EU along the way. The first Palma was the agency I built and ran in Kyiv before all that; this is its second life, in Lisbon. Different stakes — same job as yours: getting people to believe something together, and act on it.

Design, film and dev run through specialists I've worked with for years and would trust with my own brand. You get senior hands on everything and pay for zero office plants. Some names you invent; this one I earned twice.
